# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome! This README provides a concise overview of the project management approach used at OctoAcme and links to the detailed process documents in this folder. Whether you are a new team member or a returning collaborator, this guide will help you quickly understand how we plan, execute, and continuously improve our projects.

## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme employs a structured, iterative approach to project management, ensuring clarity, accountability, and continuous improvement throughout the project lifecycle. Every project begins with a robust **initiation** phase, where business needs, measurable outcomes, and key stakeholders are identified and documented in a "Project One-pager." Projects then move into a detailed **planning** stage where the team breaks down work into actionable backlog items, creates clear acceptance criteria, estimates effort, and agrees on milestones and dependencies. Kickoff meetings, risk registers, and prioritized roadmaps are standard artifacts that guide execution.

A clear delineation of roles and responsibilities ensures smooth project delivery. **Project Managers (PMs)** coordinate schedules, risks, and communications as central facilitators. **Product Managers (PdMs)** define feature priorities and success metrics, aligning the product vision with customer value. **Developers** implement features and drive code quality through peer reviews and tests, while **QA professionals** verify that outputs meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. Transparent communication is maintained through regular rituals—daily standups, weekly syncs, milestone demos, and monthly stakeholder updates—with status updates, risk escalations, and decisions documented centrally. Incident and risk communication follows standardized playbooks with clear escalation paths from team-level triage up to sponsor-level awareness.

Quality assurance is woven into every stage of OctoAcme's workflow. All new logic is accompanied by unit and integration tests, automated CI pipelines enforce quality gates (including security scans and linting), and end-to-end smoke tests are run before significant releases. **Release and deployment** are managed with checklists covering every step from staging and verification to stakeholder announcements and rollback contingency planning. Post-release **retrospectives** capture learnings and action items, driving a continuous improvement culture that enhances clarity, reliability, and team growth with every iteration.
